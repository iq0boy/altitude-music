Nawah came in with four instrumentals and a concept: make an EP that sounds both old-school hip-hop and very current. It took us three weeks to build it from scratch.

## Recording phase

We started with two vocal sessions, doubling all the key phrases on the Neumann TLM 49. The tube preamp warmth gave exactly the color he was looking for. We tracked in multitrack to keep flexibility at the mixing stage.

## The mix

Sami handled the mix. The main challenge: keeping clarity in the mid frequencies without losing the weight of the 808s. We used parallel compression on the mix bus to preserve the transients while thickening up the low end.

## What changed everything

In the third session, Nawah added an acoustic guitar on one of the tracks. It wasn't planned, but it changed everything — an organic texture that breaks from the monolithic feel of the rest. That kind of decision rarely comes from the initial plan.

The EP drops this spring. We're proud of how it turned out.
